1920

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was born to a Muslim family in the village of Tungipara located in Faridpur district's Mahakumar, Gopalganj, on March 17, 1920. ... His father Sheikh Lutfor Rahman and his mother Mosammot Sayera Khatun had four daughters and two sons, of which Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was the third child. His parents used to adoringly call him "Khoka".

1938

At the age of 18, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman married Begum Fazilatunnesa and they had three sons and two daughters.

1939

Before becoming active in student movements and politics, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman had an affinity towards sports like any other teenager. ... He possessed a special love for the game of football. A talented and renowned football player, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman spent his adolescent years playing in competitive tournaments, where he received awards for his outstanding performances.

1946

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was elected as the General Secretary of Islamia College Students Union. During this time, in the run up to the partition of India, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman played an active role in creating awareness against communal discords and riots in Bengal.

1948

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman joined the Law Department of Dhaka University and on 4th January established the East Pakistan Muslim Students League. ... On March 11, 1948, he was arrested for organizing the “Shorbo-dolio Rashtrobhasha Shongram Parishad” (an all-party coalition movement) demanding Bangla as the State Language and was held in prison for 5 days. He also started a movement aimed at securing the rights of the fourth-class employees of Dhaka University and was arrested again on September 11, 1948 and expelled.

1949

He was released from prison on 21st January, 1949. ... Right after getting out of jail, he reinitiated protests to protect the rights of the fourth-class employees. He was arrested but on 23rd January, the "East Pakistan Awami Muslim League" was established and Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was elected as the General Secretary while still in prison.

1952

On 26th January, the then Prime Minister of Pakistan Khawaja Nazimuddin declared Urdu as the only state language. ... While in captivity, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman stayed in constant touch with those waging the movement to realize Bangla as the state language. He issued key directives to make the movement a success even from the confines of his prison. On 14th February, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman started a hunger strike in prison. On 21st February, the student society came out in strike to establish Bangla as the state language in defiance of the curfew enforced by the authorities. Salam, Barkat, Rafiq, Shafiur and many others were martyred when the police fired on the procession. In a statement released from the jail, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman expressed deep sorrow for those who lost their lives and to protest against the unjust police firing, he went on a hunger strike for 17 straight days.

1956

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was again elected as the General Secretary of the party. On 16th September, ... Sheikh Mujibur Rahman joined the “Jukto Front” coalition government and received the responsibility of the ministries related to industries, trade, labor, corruption and rural assistance simultaneously. He resigned from the cabinet on May 30, 1957, in order to dedicate his time and energy completely for the party.

1964

On January 25, Awami League was revived at a meeting held at Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's residence. ... In this meeting, proposals containing the right of the general people of the country to vote and elect their own parliamentary government were accepted. In the same meeting, Maulana Abdur Rashid Tarkabagish and Sheikh Mujibur Rahman were elected President and General Secretary respectively. On 11th March, the All-Party “Sramik Parishad” was formed under the leadership of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. The Riot Resistance Committee was formed against the communal riots under the leadership of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. After the riots, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman took the initiative to prepare for an united movement against the then Pakistan’s military dictator Ayub Khan. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was arrested 14 days before the presidential election.

1966

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman presented the historic 6-point demand in a national conference of opposition parties in Lahore on 5th February. ... The proposed 6-points was the roadmap for the greater autonomy and ultimately independence for the people of East Pakistan. In order to gain support for his 6-point demand, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman travelled far and wide across the country. He was repeatedly arrested in various districts during his tours. Throughout the year, he was arrested 8 times in the first three months.

1968

The Government of Pakistan filed the ‘Agartala Conspiracy Case’ accusing Sheikh Mujibur Rahman as the main collaborator along with a total of 35 Bangla soldiers and bureaucrats for trying to destabilize Pakistan on 3rd January. ... Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was arrested again from ‘Jail Gate’ after he was released from Dhaka Central Jail and was kept imprisoned at Dhaka Cantonment. Mass movement spread around the country demanding the release of all the accused including Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. On 19th June, the trial of the accused began at Dhaka Cantonment under intense security and scrutiny.

1969

The Agartala conspiracy case resulted in a nationwide student movement and mass upsurge demanding the withdrawal of the case and the release of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. ... With continued pressure from the public, the central government on 22nd February was forced to withdraw the Agartala conspiracy case and release Sheikh Mujibur Rahman and others. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was officially awarded the title 'Bangabandhu' at a reception of a million students in a rally organized by the Central Student Sangram Parishad at the Race Course (Renamed: Suhrawardy Udyan) on 23rd February. On 5th December, the death anniversary of Shaheed Suhrawardy, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman named East Bengal as 'Bangladesh' during an Awami League discussion.

1970

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman urged the countrymen to elect Awami League in light of the 6 points. He chose 'boat' as the symbol to represent Awami League. ... When a million people died in a catastrophic cyclone in the coastal areas, on 12th November, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman suspended the election campaign and rushed to the affected areas. Awami League achieved absolute majority in the general election on 7th December, winning 167 of the 169 seats of National Assembly in East Pakistan and 305 of the 310 seats of the Provincial Council.

1971

Following Yahya Khan’s decision to not call into session the National Assembly, the aspirations for freedom of the Banglas reached a new impetus. ... From 1st March onward, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was virtually running East Pakistan as its de-facto head of government. On 7th March, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman delivered his historic speech at Race Course Meydan, considered to be his roadmap for the country’s independence. In this context, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman and Yahya Khan held a series of meetings in Dhaka between 16th and 24th March, none of which brought about any resolution. After the discussions ended on 25th March, Yahya Khan left Dhaka in the evening. On the night of 25th March, the Pakistani army launched its heinous nine-month long campaign of genocide against the unarmed Banglas. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman declared the independence of Bangladesh on 26th March at 12:20 AM.

1972

The government of Pakistan was forced to release Sheikh Mujibur Rahman under international pressure on January 8. On that very day, ...On that very Sheikh Mujibur Rahman traveled to London on his way to Dhaka. There, at a crowded press conference at his hotel in London, he spoke to the press. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman met the British Prime Minister, Edward Heath on 9th January. Prior to returning to Dhaka, he stopped over at Delhi. At the Indian airport, President V. V. Giri and Prime Minister Indira Gandhi welcomed Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. When the Father of the Nation reached Dhaka on 10th January, an unforgettable reception was given to him by millions of grateful citizens of the newest country in the world. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman went straight from the airport to the Race Course Meydan now renamed Suhrawardy Udyan, where he addressed millions of his people. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman took charge as the Prime Minister of Bangladesh and embarked on the reconstruction of a war-ravaged country on 12th January.

1974

Bangladesh received official recognition by becoming the 136th member of the United Nations on 17th September. ... On September 25, 1974, at the 29th General Assembly of the United Nations, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman delivered his speech, the first speech ever delivered at the UN in Bangla.

1975

In the pre-dawn hours of 15th August, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was assassinated by a handful of renegades backed by a larger national and international political conspiracy. ... They murdered in cold blood every member of his family except Sheikh Hasina and Sheikh Rehana, who by fortune alone were abroad at that time. Bangladesh observes August 15, 1975 as the National Mourning Day.
