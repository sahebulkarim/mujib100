1920

On March 17, 1920, the Father of the Nation Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was born to a Muslim family in Faridpur district's Gopalganj Mahakumar Tungipara village. ... His father Sheikh Lutfor Rahman and his mother Mosammot Shahara Khatun had four daughters and two sons of which Sheikh Mujib was the third child. His parents used to adoringly call him "khoka".

1938

At the age of 18, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman married Begum Fazilatunnesa and they had three sons and two daughters.

1939

Before moving into the student movements and becoming a full politician, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman had an affinity towards the playground like any other teenager. ... He possessed a special love for the game of football. A talented and renowned football player, he spent his adolescent years playing in competitive tournaments, where he received awards for his outstanding performances.

1946

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was elected as the General Secretary of Islamia College Students Union.

1948

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman joined the Law Department of Dhaka University and on 4th January established the East Pakistan Muslim Students League. ... On March 11, 1948, he was arrested for organizing the all-party coalition movement demanding Bengali as the State Language and held in prison for 5 days. He also started a movement aimed at securing the rights of the fourth-class employees of Dhaka University and was arrested again on 11th September 1948 and expelled.

1949

He was released from prison on 21st January, 1949. ... Right after getting out of jail, he reinitiated protests to protect the rights of the fourth-class employees. He was arrested but on 23rd January, the "East Pakistan Awami Muslim League" was established and Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was elected as the General Secretary while still in prison.

1952

On 26th January, Khaja Nazimuddin announced that, "Urdu will be Pakistan's state language". ... While in captivity, Sheikh Mujib urged the state language movement council to help him be released and to observe Bangla as the state language on 21st February. On 14th February Bangabandhu started a hunger strike in prison. On 21st February, the student society came out in strike to establish Bangla as the state language by refusing to abide by section 144. Salam, Barkat, Rafiq, Shafiur were martyred when the police fired on the procession. In a statement released from the jail, Bangabandhu expressed deep sorrow for those who lost their lives and to protest against the unjust police firing he went on a hunger strike for 17 straight days.

1956

In the special session of the Bangladesh Awami Muslim League on 21st October 1955, the word "Muslim" was unanimously removed from the name of the party. ... Sheikh Mujib was again elected the Secretary General of the party. Around this time, 3 people lost their lives in police firing at Chawkbazar. On 16th September, Sheikh Mujib joined the coalition government and received the responsibility of industries, trade, labor, corruption and the Ministry of Rural Assistance simultaneously. He resigned from the cabinet on 30th May, 1957, in order to dedicate his full time for the party.

1964

On January 25, Awami League was revived at a meeting held at Bangabandhu's residence. ... In this meeting, proposals containing the right of general people to get the demand of introducing parliamentary government system through voting of adult citizens of the country were accepted. In the meeting, Maulana Abdur Rashid Tarkabagish and Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib were elected President and General Secretary respectively. On 11th March, the All-Party Sramik Parishad was formed under the leadership of Bangabandhu. The Riot Resistance Committee was formed against the communal riots under the leadership of Bangabandhu. After the riots, Bangabandhu took the initiative to prepare for a united movement against Ayub. Bangabandhu was arrested 14 days before the presidential election.

1966

Bangabandhu presented a historic 6-point demand in the electoral committee on the national conference of opposition parties in Lahore on 5th February. ... The proposed 6 points was the liberation certificate of the people of Bangladesh. In order to gain support for his 6 point demand, Bangabandhu travelled far and wide across the country. He was repeatedly arrested in Sylhet, Mymensingh and Dhaka. Throughout the year, he was arrested 8 times in the first three month.

1968

On 3rd January, the Government of Pakistan filed the ‘Agartala Conspiracy Case’ accusing Bangabandhu as the main collaborator along with a total of 35 Bengali soldiers and C.S.P officers for trying to destabilize Pakistan. ... Bangabandhu was arrested again from ‘Jail Gate’ after he was released from Dhaka Central Jail and was kept imprisoned at Dhaka Cantonment. Mass movement spread around the country demanding the release of all the accused including Bangabandhu. On 19th June, the trial of the accused began at Dhaka Cantonment under intense security.

1969

The Agartala conspiracy started a nationwide student movement demanding the withdrawal of the case and the liberation of Bangabandhu. ... With the continuing pressure of the public on the central government, on 22nd February, it was forced to withdraw the Agartala conspiracy case and release Bangabandhu and others wrongly accused. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was officially awarded the title 'Bangabandhu' at the reception of 10 lakh students in a rally organized by the Central Student Sangram Parishad on Race Course (Suhrawardy Udyan) on 23rd February. On 5th December, on the death anniversary of Shaheed Suhrawardy, Bangabandhu named East Bengal as 'Bangladesh' in an Awami League discussion.

1970

Bangabandhu urged the countrymen to elect Awami League in light of the 6 points. He chose the symbol of a 'boat' to symbolize the Awami League party. ... When 10 lakh people died in the coastal area of ​​Gorky on 12th November, Bangabandhu abolished the election campaign and went to the affected areas. Awami League achieved absolute majority in the general election on 7th December. Awami League won 167 of the 169 seats in East Pakistan and 305 of the 310 seats from the provincial council.

1971

On 7th March, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman delivered a speech to a sea of crowd gathered at the Race Course, 'This struggle is our struggle for freedom, this struggle is the struggle for independence, Joy Bangla'. ... From 1 March, Bangabandhu came into power of an independent Bangladesh after a long non-cooperation movement. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman & Yahya's meeting began in the context of power transfer in Dhaka on 16th March. Bhutto came to Dhaka as well to participate in that discussion. Yahya-Mujib-Bhutto meeting went on till 24th March. After the discussions ended on 25th March, Yahya left Dhaka in the evening. On the night of 25 March, the Pakistani army attacked the innocent unarmed Bengalis and Bangabandhu declared the independence of Bangladesh on March 25th at 12:20 AM.

1972

On January 8, the government of Pakistan was forced to release Bangabandhu under international pressure. ...On that very day, Bangabandhu traveled to London. There, in London at a crowded press conference at his hotel, he spoke to the media. On 9th January, Bangabandhu met the British Prime Minister, Edward Heath. Prior to returning to Dhaka, he stopped to Delhi. At the Indian airport, President V. V. Giri and Prime Minister Mrs. Indira Gandhi welcomed Bangabandhu. When the Father of the Nation reached Dhaka on 10th January, an unforgettable reception was given to him. Bangabandhu went straight from the airport to the Race Course Maidan now renamed Suhrawardy Uddyan where millions of people awaited him. On 12th January, Bangabandhu took charge as the Prime Minister.

1974

On 25th September 1974, at the 29th General Conference of United Nation, he delivered a speech in Bangla. ... Moreover, 7 days prior to this, on 17th September, Bangladesh received recognition by becoming the 136th member of United Nation with an incredible round of applause.

1975

In the pre-dawn hours of 15th August, the noblest and greatest of Bengalis, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, the architect of Bangladesh and the Father of the nation, was assassinated by a handful of renegades backed by a larger political conspiracy.... They murdered in cold blood every member of his family except Sheikh Hasina and Sheikh Rehana, who by some fortune were at that time outside the country. August 15th, 1975 is the darkest day in Bangladesh’s history and the nation observes it as the National Mourning Day.
